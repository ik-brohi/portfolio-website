Observation and Planning

Meaningful observation and intentional planning are at the heart of quality early childhood education.

Child Observation in Practice

Throughout my placements, I conducted multiple child observations using various techniques such as anecdotal records, learning stories, jottings, and time samples. These observations helped me:

  • 📝 Understand individual developmental needs
  • 🎨 Plan engaging, play-based experiences
  • 📈 Track progress against EYLF outcomes
  • 👂 Reflect on children's voices and interests

Intentional Planning

I used a cycle of observation → analysis → planning → implementation → reflection to ensure each child’s learning journey was documented and celebrated. My planning tools included:

  • 📅 Weekly program plans
  • 📚 Learning journals
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Family input forms
  • ✅ Evaluation checklists

All activities were aligned with the EYLF learning outcomes and NQS standards.

Example: Observation-to-Planning Flow

Observation

Child A was observed using imaginative play to act out a café scenario, demonstrating verbal communication and collaboration.

Planning

Provide a dramatic play area with menus, till, and props to extend role-play and literacy exploration.

Outcome

Linked to EYLF Outcome 5: Children are effective communicators (5.1, 5.2).

Observation and planning empower educators to nurture learning with purpose, intention, and care.
