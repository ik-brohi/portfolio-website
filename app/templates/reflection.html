Reflective Practice

Reflection is at the heart of professional growth. I use it to evaluate, question, and enhance my practice to support each child’s learning journey.

Why I Reflect

Reflective practice empowers me to:

  • 🧠 Deepen understanding of children's needs and interests
  • 📈 Improve decision-making and learning outcomes
  • 🔄 Adapt planning to better respond to diverse learners
  • 🤝 Strengthen collaboration with families and educators

How I Reflect

Reflection is embedded in my daily, weekly, and long-term practice through:

  • 📒 Reflective journals after activities and routines
  • 🗣️ Team discussions and peer debriefs
  • 🧾 Reviewing learning stories and observations
  • 🧭 Alignment with EYLF outcomes and quality standards

Sample Reflection

“Today during water play, I noticed that children began negotiating roles and sharing tools. I had planned for sensory exploration, but their teamwork suggested social learning opportunities. For tomorrow, I’ll introduce cooperative challenges to support their communication and collaboration skills.”

— Linked to EYLF Outcomes 1, 4, and 5

Growth Through Reflection

My reflective practice has helped me become a more intentional, culturally responsive, and confident educator. It ensures I remain aligned with the NQS Quality Area 1 and continuously improve.

  • 📌 Recognizing bias and adapting strategies
  • 📊 Tracking development and learning trends
  • 🧘 Building self-awareness and resilience

“Reflection is not looking back — it's looking inward to move forward.”
