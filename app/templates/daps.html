Developmentally Appropriate Practices (DAPs)

Learning Through Play

Domain: Cognitive & Social Development

Description: Children learn best through active, hands-on play. This encourages exploration, imagination, and problem-solving.

Implementation: Offer sensory play stations, dramatic role play, and construction materials like blocks.

EYLF Outcome: Outcome 4 – Children are confident and involved learners

Reflection: Increased peer collaboration and language development during unstructured play.

Building Respectful Relationships

Domain: Emotional & Social Development

Description: Secure relationships provide the foundation for trust and learning.

Implementation: Use consistent greetings, acknowledge emotions, and model empathy daily.

EYLF Outcome: Outcome 1 – Children have a strong sense of identity

Reflection: Strong bonds improved classroom behavior and communication over time.

Individualised Learning

Domain: All Domains

Description: Every child learns differently. Tailoring content increases engagement and outcomes.

Implementation: Adapt storybooks or art activities to suit each child’s needs.

EYLF Outcome: Principle – High expectations and equity

Reflection: Children showed more confidence and were more willing to participate.

Culturally Responsive Practice

Domain: Cultural & Identity Development

Description: Integrating diverse cultures fosters inclusion and pride in identity.

Implementation: Incorporate multicultural books, foods, and languages in daily routine.

EYLF Outcome: Outcome 2 – Children are connected with and contribute to their world

Reflection: Children shared their home traditions with pride, boosting belonging.

Encouraging Critical Thinking

Domain: Cognitive Development

Description: Critical thinking helps children solve problems independently.

Implementation: Ask open-ended questions and present STEM-based challenges.

EYLF Outcome: Outcome 4 – Children are confident and involved learners

Reflection: Children developed persistence and creative strategies when challenged.
